Concept phase

First phase is when you and your group starts on the project planning.

Requirements - Application of processes methods, skills, knowledge and experience to achieve specific project.

Milestone - A marker or checkpoint that indicates a major goal, event, or task within a projects.

Concept Phase - The concept phase encourages and helps you take a mindful approach to your project before you formally start it.

Inception Phase

Second phase is the phase where you are working on the project

Plan - A plan is a detailed proposal for doing or achieving something complex.

Task List - A task list enables you to create a type of list, To-Do list. It also helps tell us what to do.

Project - A sequence of tasks that must be completed to attain a certain outcome.

Backlog - Backlog is a list of stuff you need to support a big project or plan.

Inception Phase - The Inception Phase is an opportunity to undertake a 'reality check' of the proposed work.

Iteration Phase

Release Phase - the process of planning, designing, scheduling, testing, deploying, and controlling software releases.

Maintenance Phase - any usual action, activity, expense, replacement, adjustment or repair taken to retain a project.

Unit Test - Is an important aspect to software, and it has its own individual testing.

Retirement phase

The ending of the project

Retirement Phase - The Retirement Phase covers system withdrawal, system decommissioning, system disposal, and migration of data.

Project Management methods

agile - Agile management is the application of the principles of Agile software development and Lean Management to various management processes, particularly product development.

waterfall - A linear application developer model that uses right phases, on ends and the next begins.
